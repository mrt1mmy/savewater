Welcome to our environmental awareness games! Before you start playing, let's talk about why watershed protection matters.

What is a Watershed?

A watershed is an area of land where all water drains to a common point - like a river, lake, or ocean. Everything that happens on the land affects the water quality downstream.

Why Watersheds Matter:

  • Drinking Water: Watersheds provide clean drinking water for communities
  • Wildlife Habitat: Healthy watersheds support diverse ecosystems
  • Flood Control: Proper watershed management reduces flooding risks
  • Water Quality: Healthy watersheds mean cleaner rivers, lakes, and oceans
  • Recreation: Clean water supports fishing, swimming, and boating

How These Games Help:

Each game teaches simple habits that protect our watersheds:

  • Save Water Game: Learn water conservation to reduce strain on water systems
  • Rain Barrel Game: Understand rainwater harvesting to reduce runoff pollution
  • Recycling Quiz: Learn proper recycling to prevent waste from entering waterways

Remember: Every drop counts! Small actions add up to create big change for our watersheds.

Save Water & Toothbrush Game

How to Play

Click the "Turn On" button to start the water flow. Try to use as little water as possible while brushing your teeth. Click "Turn Off" when you're done to see how much water you used. Try to beat your best score!

Why This Helps the Environment

Turning off the tap while brushing teeth can save up to 8 gallons of water per day! This simple habit conserves freshwater resources and reduces the energy needed to treat and pump water.

Watershed Connection

Water conservation reduces the amount of water withdrawn from rivers and aquifers, helping maintain healthy water levels in our watersheds for wildlife and ecosystems.

Rain Barrel Game

How to Play

Click "Start Game" to begin. Use the left and right arrow keys to move the barrel and catch as many raindrops as possible in 30 seconds. Each raindrop you collect represents water saved for gardening and other outdoor uses.

Why This Helps the Environment

Rain barrels collect rainwater that would otherwise run off into storm drains. This reduces water pollution from runoff and conserves treated drinking water for outdoor use. A single rain barrel can save about 1,300 gallons of water during peak summer months!

Watershed Connection

Rainwater harvesting reduces stormwater runoff, which carries pollutants from streets into our waterways. This helps keep our watersheds clean and reduces erosion.

Recycling Quiz

How to Play

For each item shown, decide whether it should go in the recycling bin or the trash. You'll get feedback on your answers to help you learn proper recycling practices.

Why This Helps the Environment

Proper recycling reduces landfill waste, conserves natural resources, saves energy, and reduces pollution. When we recycle correctly, we help create a circular economy where materials are reused instead of discarded.

Watershed Connection

Proper recycling prevents waste from entering waterways through runoff or improper disposal. It also reduces the need for new materials, which decreases mining and manufacturing pollution that can contaminate watersheds.
